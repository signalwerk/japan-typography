Reading unspoken elements in Japanese Typography

An Observation

This article provides a formal and analytical exploration of the unspoken elements in Japanese typography, from the viewpoint of a professional with a background in Latin typography. It dives into the subtle, purely typographic and therefore not only in written form relevant features of Japanese script, emphasizing their significance in the broader context of typographic design and communication. The aim is to provide an understanding of these elements and showcasing how they contribute to the overall aesthetics of written Japanese. [^1][^aber]
